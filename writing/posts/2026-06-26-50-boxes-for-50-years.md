*Fifty boxes. Fifty years. The math was deliberate. The results were honest.*

![Fifty boxes before opening](images/2026-06-26-50-boxes-for-50-years/box-stack.jpg "The full stack before any of them were opened")

Turning 50 felt like a reasonable excuse to do something excessive. The idea was simple: one box for every year. Not random boxes — fifty that mapped to the things I actually collect. Hockey cards, Star Trek, Star Wars, Doctor Who, Marvel, DC. A snapshot of the collection at this point in life.

I broke them over the weeks around my birthday. Privately, the way I open everything. No camera, no live audience. Just boxes.

Here's what happened.

---

## The list

The full fifty broke down like this: 23 hockey (including a full case of 2025 Team Canada Juniors across 12 boxes), 9 Marvel, 8 Star Trek, 3 DC, 3 Doctor Who, 2 Star Wars, and 2 that don't fit a clean bucket — Topps Chrome Neon and Phantom.

That distribution isn't accidental. It reflects where the collection actually sits. Team Canada is the anchor. Rittenhouse products — Trek and Who — are consistent enough to keep coming back to. Marvel and DC are franchise collecting, not set chasing.

---

## Hockey: one story, two very different chapters

The Team Canada case was the highlight of the entire break. A full case gives you enough volume to actually feel what a set has to offer, and the 2025 Juniors set delivered. Gavin McKenna is the standout name in the product and the pulls reflected that.

From the case:

- McKenna Pride of the Program Gold Foil /35
- McKenna Pride of the Program Exclusives /100
- McKenna License to Ice Red /25
- Easton Cowan SP Patch /25
- Bjarnason Patch — not an SP, but one of the nicer patches I've pulled for a Canada collector

That's a good result. McKenna is worth chasing in this set, and the patches were cleaner than expected.

The other 11 hockey boxes told a different story. Across three OPC Platinum years, UD Premier, and multiple Allures, finding five cards worth mentioning was a genuine struggle.

What I pulled:

- Vasilevskiy Auto (23-24 Platinum)
- Buium Rookie Auto (25-26 Platinum)
- Schaefer Violet Pixels (25-26 Platinum)
- Pettersson Mega Patch (23-24 Premier)
- Kelemen Focus Patch (23-24 Premier) — no idea who he is, but the patch is nice

Eleven boxes. That's the reality of breaking hockey by the box at this price point. Cases are too expensive to absorb regularly. Individual boxes don't move the needle. It's a math problem without a great answer.

## Star Wars and Star Trek: small boxes, good results

Two Star Wars boxes is not a lot, but the Chrome Mandalorian and Galaxy Chrome both delivered. Getting a sketch from two boxes is a good rate.

- Grogu sketch by Andrew Fry (SW Chrome)
- Paul Sun-Hyung Lee Auto (SW Chrome)

Star Trek had a few boxes that were quiet, but the hits that landed were the right ones.

- Anson Mount Auto (Strange New Worlds)
- Painted sketch by Charles Hall (Discovery)
- Robert Beltran Auto (Connections)
- Kirk Memorabilia (Connections)

The Mount auto was one I needed. Connections remains one of the most reliable boxes in the hobby — it almost never misses. The Discovery sketch is a nice piece; Hall's painted style reads differently than a pen sketch and it works.

![Grogu sketch by Andrew Fry](images/2026-06-26-50-boxes-for-50-years/grogu-sketch-fry.jpg "Grogu sketch by Andrew Fry — SW Chrome Mandalorian")
![Discovery painted sketch by Charles Hall](images/2026-06-26-50-boxes-for-50-years/discovery-sketch-hall.jpg "Painted sketch by Charles Hall — ST Discovery")

Three Doctor Who boxes produced one hit worth naming but it was the right one: a Jenna Coleman auto. She's my favourite companion. That's the kind of pull that makes a box worth it regardless of the rest.

## Marvel: volume with mixed returns

Nine boxes is a real commitment to a franchise. The results were uneven.

The Studio Series boxes — Series One and Series Two — each produced a Hayley Atwell auto. Back to back. That's a funny result and genuinely good cards.

- Series One: Hayley Atwell What If? Auto
- Series Two: Hayley Atwell Doctor Strange Auto

![Hayley Atwell What If? Auto](images/2026-06-26-50-boxes-for-50-years/hayley-atwell-s1-auto.jpg "Hayley Atwell What If? Auto — Marvel Studios Series One")
![Hayley Atwell Doctor Strange Auto](images/2026-06-26-50-boxes-for-50-years/hayley-atwell-s2-auto.jpg "Hayley Atwell Doctor Strange Auto — Marvel Studios Series Two")

Topps Chrome Deadpool had the most interesting result in the worst possible way. The top pull was a Wild Thing parallel /5 — great hit on paper. Wrong character for the collection. The patch card at /50 is a nicer piece but doesn't change the math on whether Chrome Deadpool is worth buying again at that price point. It's probably not.

The rest of the Marvel boxes — Allure MCU, Masterpieces Platinum, Masterpieces XL, Falcon & Winter Soldier, Hawkeye, What If? — produced nothing notable. No Hawkeye hits to speak of, which is a miss given that's a primary target in the collection.

## DC and the misc boxes: where the fun actually was

This surprised me. Batman Animated Series, Batman Metal Universe, and Fleer Ultra Superman were less about chasing value and more about genuinely enjoying what came out.

- Batman TAS Red Outburst — Baby-Doll /25
- Superman Medallion — Doomsday /10

![Doomsday Medallion /10](images/2026-06-26-50-boxes-for-50-years/doomsday-medallion.jpg "Superman Medallion — Doomsday /10 — Fleer Ultra Superman")
![Batman TAS Baby-Doll Red Outburst /25](images/2026-06-26-50-boxes-for-50-years/baby-doll-outburst.jpg "Batman TAS Red Outburst — Baby-Doll /25")

The Doomsday medallion is a standout piece. The TAS parallel is exactly the kind of card that looks better in hand than any scan conveys.

Topps Chrome Neon was the most interesting box to open because I walked in without expectations or price anchors.

- Nemo Auto (Chrome Neon)
- Buzz Lightyear Parallel /25 (Chrome Neon)

I don't know the market on most of these and that turned out to be an asset. The only question was whether I liked the card. On that metric, Chrome Neon won.

Phantom was a gift from a friend. Opening it, my first thought was that I should have leaned harder into 90s stuff for this break — the retro feel would have fit. Then I looked at what came out and reconsidered. Maybe it's fine that I didn't.

---

## What the break taught me

Team Canada and the misc boxes were the most enjoyable part of fifty boxes.

Team Canada works because a full case gives you the volume to chase something specific, the set has a player worth targeting right now in McKenna, and the product quality is there. I'll keep breaking it.

The Rittenhouse products — Trek and Who — are reliable and franchise-appropriate. Those stay too.

The misc stuff works because value stops being the lens. You pull something, you either like it or you don't. I liked most of what came out of Neon and Phantom and the DC boxes, and I hadn't planned to.

What the rest of the hockey and most of the Marvel boxes confirmed is something I'd already started to think: breaking by the box is a diminishing return at current price points, and I don't enjoy it enough to keep absorbing it. The plan coming out of this is to reduce significantly — probably to Team Canada, Rittenhouse products, and Upper Deck DC, with the occasional exception.

Fifty boxes clarified more than it surprised. That's probably the right outcome for a project like this.

— Josh Glover is a collector based in Ottawa. He collects Star Wars, Star Trek, Doctor Who, hockey cards, and a few other things he can't fully explain.
