*The maple leaf shows up in the collection more than I planned for it to.*

At some point I stopped being surprised by it. Pull out the Team Canada hockey cards on Canada Day — that's the obvious one. But look a little wider and the thread keeps going. Canadian artists, Canadian characters, Canadian celebrities across half a dozen different hobbies. Turns out if you put a maple leaf on something, I'll probably end up owning it.

## The artists

Ken Lashley is one of my favourite comic artists working today. He's also Canadian, which feels entirely consistent with the way he operates. I've got a significant amount of work from him — commissions, originals, pieces across a lot of different characters — accumulated across years of conventions. What I didn't anticipate was that it would eventually extend to my youngest son.

Ken did design work on a line of goalie masks. My son ended up wearing one. That's the kind of intersection that happens when you keep showing up at conventions long enough — the collecting and the rest of your life find each other. We've got a photo of the two of them together with the mask, and that one doesn't leave the collection.

![Ken Lashley and my son with the goalie mask](images/2026-07-01-collecting-canadian/lashley-goalie-mask.jpg "Ken Lashley with my son and the mask he designed")

Nick Bradshaw is the other. Same story in the broad strokes — Canadian, convention regular, someone whose work I kept coming back to until I had a real collection of it. Commissions, originals. The work is the obvious draw, but what keeps me at the table with both of them is harder to articulate. There's a way they carry themselves at shows that I'd call genuinely Canadian without needing to get into what that means. If you've spent enough time at conventions you know the difference.

## Team Canada

This one needs no explanation on the surface. Team Canada hockey has been the anchor of the collection for years and I share from it constantly.

For Canada Day I'm pulling out three pieces that sit at the top of the whole collection. From the 2015 Team Canada Master Collection: three logo cards, each a 1/1, each containing a patch of the Hockey Canada logo. The reason those patches exist at all is that the logo wasn't permitted to be worn at the Olympics — so it was sewn on the inside of their jerseys instead. Game-used. One of a kind. Getzlaf, Pronger, Bergeron. Three Olympic gold medallists.

The set itself came in a wooden collector's box with the Hockey Canada logo on it. I still have it. It's the right home for those cards.

I collect Team Canada because I love the program. I'm also an Olympics obsessive. The Hockey Canada logo means something specific to me beyond the product. These three cards hit every one of those notes at once, which is why they're genuinely the hardest things in the collection to look at without feeling something.

## Wolverine

He's Wolverine. He's also Canadian. That second part carries more weight for me than I used to acknowledge.

I was a Wolverine fan before I thought much about it. Eventually I noticed how much his Canadian identity is actually built into the character, not bolted on, and that I'd been drawn to it without naming it. A maple leaf on the cover is rarely a coincidence in my collection.

## The rest of it

Beyond hockey there are Canadian names scattered through the auto collection. Nathan Fillion — met him at a show. Ryan Reynolds. Ross Rebagliati. Trish Stratus. Larry Walker. Jacques Villeneuve. Different sports, different careers, completely different eras. All Canadian, all in the same collection.

## What it adds up to

I didn't plan a Canadian-themed collection. I planned to collect things I actually cared about. Turns out I care about a lot of Canadian things — artists who show up a certain way, characters who happen to have the right passport, names that mean something to me because of what they represent.

The maple leaf keeps appearing because I keep letting it in. That's the whole explanation.
