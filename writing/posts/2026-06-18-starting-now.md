In March I was laid off. I'm not going to spend a lot of time on what happened or why — partly because I don't fully know, and partly because it doesn't change anything. What I know is that three months of telling the story quietly, in pieces, to whoever asked, is enough of that.

I turn 50 on June 18. Those two things arriving close together will do something to you.

I've spent a lot of years having thoughts I didn't say, and not enough time saying the ones that mattered. Some of that is introversion. Some of it is that the right moment never seemed to show up. Some of it is that I wasn't sure anyone needed to hear it.

I'm less convinced of that last part now. And honestly — I've realized I need to say some of this regardless. If it lands with someone, that's enough. If it doesn't, I still needed to say it.

I'm going to write here. About collecting — that's not going anywhere. But also about the things I've been sitting on. Mental health. Getting older. Culture, politics without calling it politics, the way social media makes everything worse and we keep using it anyway. The stuff men especially don't tend to say out loud.

Not all at once. But starting now.

— Josh
