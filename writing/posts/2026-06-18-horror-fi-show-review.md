Geeked Out Events ran Horror-Fi at the Nepean Sportsplex June 13-14, Ottawa's first show built entirely around horror and sci-fi. One hall, full weekend, 10am-4pm both days.

![Horror-Fi convention floor at the Nepean Sportsplex](/writing/images/2026-06-horror-fi-show-review/venue-floor.png "Horror-Fi Banner, June 13-14")

The all-vendor focus mattered more than I expected. Every booth had to be 100% horror or sci-fi, no exceptions. At a general pop culture show you walk past the same wrestling merch and anime prints table after table. Here the floor actually turned up things I hadn't seen, even in categories I don't normally stop for.

The guest list wasn't really my lane, horror isn't a strong interest for me, but it's a solid lineup for a first-year show:

- Lisa Wilcox - Nightmare on Elm Street 4 & 5, also a Star Trek: TNG credit
- Julian Richings - Death in Supernatural, also The Witch and Cube
- Ty Olsson - Supernatural, War for the Planet of the Apes, Thanksgiving
- Osric Chau - Supernatural, Star Trek: Discovery, The 100
- Michael Chan - Star Trek: Discovery, Murdoch Mysteries; autograph proceeds went to the Dup15q Alliance
- Ronn Sutton - comic artist since the early '70s, known for his Elvira work, Joe Shuster Hall of Fame inductee
- Janet Hetherington - wrote Elvira for Claypool Comics for six years
- Brett Kelly - Ottawa filmmaker, 40+ low-budget creature features

Decent range for a chapter one. Shows they can land real names before the event has any track record to point to.

I spent most of my time on the sci-fi side, digging through toys and magazines. Came away with some Boba Fett pieces, including a US Target-exclusive Funko Pop I hadn't seen yet from one of the regular Pop vendors. Picked up a couple of action figures I already own sealed, nice to have loose versions to actually display without opening the ones I'm keeping packaged. Also dug up a couple of Boba Fett magazine covers, the kind of find a smaller, focused show makes possible.

![Boba Fett pickups from Horror-Fi](/writing/images/2026-06-horror-fi-show-review/pickup-1.jpg "Part of the haul")
![Boba Fett magazine covers from Horror-Fi](/writing/images/2026-06-horror-fi-show-review/pickup-2.jpg)

Had a good conversation with a vendor selling 3D-printed pieces alongside toys. One of the real advantages of a smaller show is the room to actually talk between booths instead of just moving with the crowd.

Horror isn't where my interest is, so a good chunk of the floor wasn't built for me. That's fine. The sci-fi side was worth the trip on its own, and that's really the point of a show like this: a few hours where nobody has to downplay whatever they're into, horror included, even if it's not mine.

Heard from a vendor that Saturday sales were soft. Sunday wasn't too busy either, but traffic looked reasonable. Could be newness, could be advertising. I only knew about the show because I'm on the organizer's mailing list, so some more advertising might be needed. Still hoping to see this grow going forward. It'd actually be a good venue to move some of my sci-fi stuff that doesn't fit the collection anymore and never seems to be the "prime" stuff at the shows I usually do.

— Josh Glover is a collector based in Ottawa. He collects Star Wars, Star Trek, Doctor Who, hockey cards, and a few other things he can't fully explain.
